PROLOGUE
Cupid, accompanied by a group of shepherds, sings of the power of Love.

ACT I
They are seen embracing on a couch. The sound of a hunt is heard, and she eventually persuads the reluctant he to join in the boar chase.

ACT II
They discuss the arts of love and teach a group of infant Cupids how to spell. After a dance, they enter and perform a suite of dances during which the Cupids adorn Venus.

ACT III
She is distraught as he is brought in, mortally wounded by the boar. She implores the gods to have pity on her, but he dies. The Cupids sing a chorus of mourning.
